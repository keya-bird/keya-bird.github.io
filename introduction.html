Introduction To My Portfolio

November 25th, 2023 by Keya Birdsbill

Welcome to my portfolio. My portfolio is using using custom components and layouts that are supported and styled with Bootstrap. Basic typography, lists, tables, images, code, and more are all supported as expected and in some cases slightly modified to fit my look and feel.

As part of an MIT homework assignment that constitutes 40% of my grade, I have developed a portfolio consisting of: a landing page, video introduction of myself, a navigation bar, a projects page that interlinks with my GitHub repositories, a contact me section and Bootstrap typography and layout.

Learning

Here's a quick snapshot of what I've learned while developing my portfolio:

Utilizing modular HTML

Creating a fully functional reusable component

Fetching the content of an external HTML file

Attribute Removal and Recursion

I am incredibly grateful for the invaluable learning experience I've gained through the MIT Course Coding for Women. This opportunity has been immensely rewarding, and I continue to learn and grow from it even now. One of my long-standing curiosities was understanding how to dynamically include content from an external HTML file into another. Developing this portfolio presented a technical challenge that allowed me to master this technique, along with many others.

Project Site Structure

Included in my portfolio project are these items:

  • A landing page with Video Intro.

A personal note

December 23, 2020 by Keya Birdsbill

I lead a busy and fulfilling life, balancing the demands of family, work, and my passion for technology. My dedication to my family is unwavering, as I raise our four children and care for our beloved furry companions. This portfolio showcases my diverse talents and adaptability throughout my career. I have thrived in roles such as Network Operations Analyst and Web Analyst, and I am now embarking on an exciting journey to learn JavaScript. My expertise is constantly evolving and expanding. Alongside my full-time job, I also actively manage two successful businesses - KeyaTech and Water People Tours. I believe that my husband and I share an incredible work ethic and entrepreneurial spirit, qualities we strive to instill in our children. Life is a lifelong learning process, and I believe it's essential to be a driven and passionate individual. We must constantly challenge ourselves to grow and embrace new opportunities. The Professional Certificate in Coding: For Women at MIT Emeritus has provided me with a wonderful opportunity to learn and expand my skills, an experience I am truly grateful for.

About

As an Indigenous Native American woman and the proud wife and mother of four incredible children, I am deeply driven to overcome poverty and prejudice through the pursuit of higher education in technology. My passion for web technologies and technology as a whole fuels my determination to pave a path for my children and future generations, empowering them to achieve success and break down the barriers that have long burdened our communities.
